# Union Relations by Industry

Major unions, membership, recent campaigns, and industry relationships.

## Major Unions by Sector

### Transportation

```
TEAMSTERS (IBT)
├── Members: 1.3M
├── President: Sean O'Brien (2022)
├── Sectors: Freight, UPS, warehousing, Amazon
├── Recent Activity:
│   ├── UPS Contract (2023): Historic deal, 340K workers
│   │   ├── Top driver pay: $41+/hour
│   │   └── Eliminated two-tier wages
│   ├── Amazon Division: Absorbed ALU (June 2024)
│   │   ├── JFK8 Staten Island: ALU-IBT Local 1
│   │   └── Dec 2024: "Largest nationwide Amazon strike"
│   └── Yellow Freight: Bankruptcy (2023)
├── Employers:
│   ├── UPS: 340,000 members
│   ├── ABF Freight
│   ├── YRC (bankrupt)
│   └── Amazon: Organizing
└── Position: #1 trucking/logistics union

INTERNATIONAL LONGSHOREMEN'S ASSOCIATION (ILA)
├── Members: 65,000
├── President: Harold Daggett
├── Sector: East/Gulf Coast ports
├── 2024 Strike:
│   ├── Ports: 36 (Maine to Texas)
│   ├── Duration: 3 days (Oct 2024)
│   ├── Impact: $3.7-4.5B daily
│   └── Result: 62% wage increase over 6 years

INTERNATIONAL LONGSHORE & WAREHOUSE UNION (ILWU)
├── Members: 50,000

RAILROAD UNIONS:

├── IBEW (Electrical): Rail electrical

```

### Manufacturing/Auto

```
UNITED AUTO WORKERS (UAW)
├── Members: 400,000+
├── President: Shawn Fain (2023)
├── Sectors: Auto, aerospace, agriculture equipment
├── 2023 "Stand Up Strike":
│   ├── Target: GM, Ford, Stellantis
│   ├── Workers: 46,000
│   ├── Duration: 6 weeks
│   ├── Result: $23B+ in gains
│   │   ├── 25%+ wage increases
│   │   ├── Top wage: $42/hour by 2028
│   │   ├── COLA restored
│   │   └── Right to strike over plant closures
│   └── Impact: Most significant auto victory in decades
├── Organizing Drives:
│   ├── VW Chattanooga: Won (April 2024, 73%)
│   ├── Mercedes Alabama: Lost (May 2024, 44%)
│   └── Tesla, Toyota, Honda: Ongoing
├── Employers:
│   ├── GM: 46,000 members
│   ├── Ford: 57,000 members
│   ├── Stellantis: 43,000 members
│   └── John Deere: 10,000 (struck 2021)
└── Status: Resurgent, aggressive organizing

INTERNATIONAL ASSOCIATION OF MACHINISTS (IAM)
├── Members: 600,000
├── President: Brian Bryant
├── Sectors: Aerospace, airlines, rail, defense
├── 2024 Boeing Strike:
│   ├── Duration: 7 weeks (Sep-Nov 2024)
│   ├── Workers: 33,000
│   ├── Result: 38-43% wage increase over 4 years
│   ├── Bonus: $12,000 ratification
│   ├── Failed: Pension restoration
│   └── Cost to Boeing: $5-6.4B estimated
├── Employers:
│   ├── Boeing: 33,000 members
│   ├── Lockheed Martin: 35,000
│   ├── United Airlines
│   └── Delta (limited)
└── Position: #1 aerospace union

UNITED STEELWORKERS (USW)
├── Members: 850,000
├── President: David McCall
├── Sectors: Steel, paper, oil refining, mining
├── Employers:
│   ├── Cleveland-Cliffs: Major
│   ├── US Steel: Historic (Nippon Steel deal contested)
│   ├── Oil refiners: Marathon, Valero, Phillips
│   └── Paper: International Paper
├── US Steel Position: Opposed Nippon Steel acquisition
└── Status: Key voice on trade policy
```
