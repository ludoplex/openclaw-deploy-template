## Common Scams to Watch For

### Tech Support Scams

- [ ] Microsoft/Apple will NEVER call you first
- [ ] Pop-ups saying "Your computer is infected" are fake
- [ ] Never give remote access to unsolicited callers
- [ ] Never buy gift cards to "pay" for tech support

### Phone Scams

- [ ] IRS only contacts by mail first
- [ ] Police do not call to collect bail via gift cards
- [ ] Your grandchild will not call asking for money in secret
- [ ] Utility companies do not demand immediate gift card payment

### Email/Online Scams

- [ ] Check sender email address carefully (not just display name)
- [ ] Banks do not ask for passwords via email
- [ ] Prize winnings you did not enter are fake
- [ ] "Urgent" requests for money are usually scams

---

## What to Do

### If You Get a Suspicious Call

1. Hang up
2. Do NOT press any numbers
3. Call the company directly using a number you look up yourself
4. Tell a trusted friend or family member

### If Someone Has Remote Access

1. Shut down your computer immediately
2. Unplug from internet
3. Bring it to us for a checkup ($40)
4. Change your important passwords from another device

### If You Already Paid

1. Contact your bank immediately
2. Report to FTC: ReportFraud.ftc.gov
3. File a police report
4. Bring us your device to check for malware

## Remember

- Legitimate companies do not demand gift cards
- When in doubt, hang up and call us
- It is OK to say "no" and hang up
- Scammers create urgency to prevent you from thinking
