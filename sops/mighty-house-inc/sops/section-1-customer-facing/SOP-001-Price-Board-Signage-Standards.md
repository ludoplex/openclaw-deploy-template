# SOP-001: Official Price Board and Signage Standards

## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the official pricing structure, signage requirements, and display standards for all Mighty House Inc. Computer Store locations. Consistent, visible pricing builds customer trust, reduces friction at checkout, and ensures chain-wide compliance with advertised rates.

**Key Objectives:**
- Ensure identical pricing across all locations
- Maintain professional, readable signage
- Prevent unauthorized price modifications
- Support transparent customer communication

## 2. Scope

- **Applies to:** Store Managers, Assistant Managers, Front Desk Staff, All Employees
- **Locations:** All Mighty House Inc. Computer Store locations
- **Frequency:** Daily verification; quarterly signage audit
- **Authority:** Only Regional Manager or above may authorize price changes

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Price Board | The official 24x36" printed sign displaying all service prices |
| Service Menu | Digital or printed list of services with descriptions |
| Signage Audit | Quarterly inspection of all posted prices for accuracy |
| Price Lock | Period during which prices cannot be changed (2025-FINAL) |
| Labor Rate | The service fee charged for technician work time |
| Parts Cost | Customer-paid cost for replacement components (pass-through) |

## 4. Prerequisites

- [ ] Store location is open and operational
- [ ] Official Price Board has been printed and delivered
- [ ] Manager has access to current pricing master file
- [ ] Display hardware (frame, mounting) is installed
- [ ] Lighting for signage area is functional

## 6. Procedure Steps

### Step 1: Price Board Installation

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Actor** | Store Manager |
| **Input** | Official Price Board (24x36"), mounting hardware, level |
| **Action** | Install Price Board in designated customer-visible location |
| **Output** | Price Board mounted, level, and illuminated |
| **Verification** | Readable from 10 feet; no glare; all text visible |

**Detailed Instructions:**

1. Identify the designated Price Board location (typically behind front counter or in waiting area)
2. Ensure wall surface is clean and can support mounting hardware
3. Use level to mark mounting points
4. Install frame or mounting hardware per manufacturer instructions
5. Insert Official Price Board into frame
6. Verify board is level and secure
7. Adjust lighting to eliminate glare and shadows
8. Take photo for compliance file

**Location Requirements:**
- Visible from customer entry point
- Readable from main service counter
- Minimum 4 feet from floor (bottom edge)
- Not obstructed by displays, equipment, or traffic flow

---

### Step 2: Daily Price Board Verification

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Actor** | Opening Staff Member |
| **Input** | Price Board Verification Checklist |
| **Action** | Inspect Price Board during opening procedures |
| **Output** | Completed verification checklist |
| **Verification** | All items checked; issues escalated immediately |

**Detailed Instructions:**

1. Approach Price Board from customer perspective (10 feet away)
2. Verify board is:
   - [ ] Present and not removed
   - [ ] Clean (no dust, smudges, or damage)
   - [ ] Level and securely mounted
   - [ ] Properly illuminated
   - [ ] Free of unauthorized modifications
3. Check for any handwritten additions or crossed-out prices (NOT ALLOWED)
4. Verify current date matches effective date on board
5. Initial daily checklist
6. Report any issues to Store Manager immediately

**Escalation Triggers:**
- Missing or damaged Price Board
- Any unauthorized price modifications
- Lighting failure affecting readability
- Customer complaints about pricing visibility

---

### Step 3: Price Quote Delivery to Customers

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Actor** | Front Desk Staff / Technician |
| **Input** | Customer service request, Price Board |
| **Action** | Quote price directly from Official Price Board |
| **Output** | Verbal price quote matching posted price |
| **Verification** | Customer acknowledges price before work begins |

**Detailed Instructions:**

1. Listen to customer service request
2. Identify appropriate service from Price Board
3. Point to Price Board while quoting:
   > "For [service], our price is $[amount] as shown on our board."
4. Explain any applicable conditions:
   - Time caps (e.g., "2-hour cap on virus removal")
   - Parts costs (e.g., "$119 plus the cost of the screen")
   - Guarantees (e.g., "No data recovered means no charge")
5. Mention discount eligibility if customer qualifies:
   > "If you're 65 or older, you qualify for 10% off labor Monday through Thursday."
6. Document quoted price on intake form
7. Obtain customer signature acknowledging price

**Prohibited Practices:**
- Quoting prices from memory without referencing board
- Offering unauthorized discounts
- Rounding prices up or down
- Promising prices not on the official board

---

### Step 4: Handling Price Disputes

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Actor** | Store Manager / Front Desk Staff |
| **Input** | Customer price complaint or dispute |
| **Action** | Reference Official Price Board; escalate if needed |
| **Output** | Resolution or escalation to Regional Manager |
| **Verification** | Customer concern addressed; documentation complete |

**Detailed Instructions:**

1. Listen to customer concern without interruption
2. Walk customer to Price Board:
   > "Let me show you our official pricing."
3. Point to relevant service and price
4. If customer claims different advertised price:
   - Ask where they saw the price (website, flyer, etc.)
   - Document the claimed source
   - Explain: "Our official prices are posted here. Let me check with my manager."
5. Manager decision tree:
   - If legitimate advertising error: Honor lower price, report to Regional
   - If customer misunderstanding: Politely clarify, offer alternatives
   - If customer remains dissatisfied: Offer contact info for Regional Manager
6. Document all price disputes in incident log

---

### Step 5: Quarterly Signage Audit

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Actor** | Store Manager + Regional Manager |
| **Input** | Signage Audit Checklist, current master price file |
| **Action** | Complete comprehensive signage inspection |
| **Output** | Signed audit report; corrective actions if needed |
| **Verification** | All signage matches master price file exactly |

**Detailed Instructions:**

1. Schedule audit during low-traffic period
2. Print current master price file from corporate system
3. Compare every price on Price Board to master file:
   - [ ] All services listed
   - [ ] All prices correct
   - [ ] All descriptions accurate
   - [ ] Discount terms correct
4. Inspect secondary signage:
   - [ ] Window signs
   - [ ] Counter cards
   - [ ] Digital displays (if applicable)
   - [ ] Website (spot check)
5. Check for unauthorized signage:
   - [ ] No handwritten price signs
   - [ ] No outdated promotional materials
   - [ ] No competitor comparisons
6. Document findings on audit form
7. Both managers sign audit report
8. Submit to corporate within 48 hours
9. Complete corrective actions within 7 days

---

### Step 6: Price Board Replacement

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Actor** | Store Manager |
| **Input** | Damaged board or corporate price update notice |
| **Action** | Order and install replacement Price Board |
| **Output** | New Price Board installed; old board destroyed |
| **Verification** | New board matches current master price file |

**Detailed Instructions:**

1. Identify need for replacement:
   - Physical damage (tears, fading, water damage)
   - Corporate price update notification
   - Quarterly refresh schedule
2. Submit replacement request through corporate portal
3. Upon receipt of new board:
   - Verify all prices match notification/master file
   - Schedule installation during low-traffic period
4. Remove old Price Board:
   - Do NOT discard in customer-accessible trash
   - Shred or destroy to prevent misuse
5. Install new board per Step 1 procedures
6. Document replacement in maintenance log
7. Take photo of new installation for compliance file

## 8. Exception Handling

### Exception 1: Price Board Missing or Severely Damaged

**Trigger:** Price Board cannot be displayed to customers

**Resolution:**
1. Immediately print temporary price sheet from corporate system
2. Post in clear sleeve at counter level
3. Notify Regional Manager within 1 hour
4. Order replacement board same day
5. Document in incident log
6. Temporary signage must be removed within 72 hours of replacement arrival

### Exception 2: Customer Shows Different Advertised Price

**Trigger:** Customer presents advertisement, website screenshot, or coupon with different price

**Resolution:**
1. Verify the source is legitimate Mighty House Inc. material
2. Check date on material (may be expired)
3. If valid current promotion: Honor the advertised price
4. If expired: Explain expiration, offer current pricing
5. If fraudulent/competitor material: Politely decline, show official board
6. Report all instances to Regional Manager for tracking

### Exception 3: Service Not Listed on Price Board

**Trigger:** Customer requests service not shown on official Price Board

**Resolution:**
1. Check if service falls under existing category (quote that price)
2. If truly custom/unlisted:
   - Explain: "Let me get a custom quote from our technician."
   - Technician provides estimate based on time and complexity
   - Manager approves quote before presenting to customer
3. Document custom quotes for potential addition to future price lists
4. Never invent prices - always get manager approval

### Exception 4: Discount Dispute (Day of Week)

**Trigger:** Customer requests senior/veteran discount on Friday, Saturday, or Sunday

**Resolution:**
1. Politely explain: "Our senior and veteran discounts are available Monday through Thursday."
2. Offer alternatives:
   - "You're welcome to return Monday-Thursday for the discount."
   - "Would you like to schedule the service for [next eligible day]?"
3. Do NOT make exceptions - this maintains fairness for all customers
4. If customer escalates, refer to Store Manager

## 9. Signage Specifications

### Primary Price Board

| Specification | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| Size | 24 inches x 36 inches |
| Material | Foam board or rigid PVC (minimum 3mm) |
| Finish | Matte laminate (anti-glare) |
| Mounting | Professional frame or standoffs |
| Colors | Per brand guidelines (contact corporate) |
| Font | Sans-serif, minimum 24pt for prices |
| Replacement | Annual or upon damage/price change |

### Secondary Signage (Optional)

| Type | Size | Location | Content |
|------|------|----------|---------|
| Counter Card | 5x7" | Service counter | Top 5 services |
| Window Cling | 8.5x11" | Front window | "Repairs Starting at $49" |
| A-Frame | 24x36" | Sidewalk (where permitted) | Daily special or general services |

### Prohibited Signage

- Handwritten price signs
- Competitor price comparisons
- "Negotiable" or "Ask for discount" language
- Prices written on whiteboards
- Unofficial promotional materials
- Crossed-out prices with new prices written

## 11. Compliance Notes

- **Corporate Policy:** All prices are set by Mighty House Inc. corporate. Individual locations have no authority to modify prices without written Regional Manager approval.
- **Truth in Advertising:** Posted prices must match all advertising, website listings, and verbal quotes. Violations may result in disciplinary action.
- **Price Lock Period:** The 2025-FINAL price schedule is locked through December 31, 2025. No changes will be made without 30-day advance notice to all locations.
- **Audit Requirement:** Quarterly signage audits are mandatory. Failure to complete audits may affect store performance ratings.
